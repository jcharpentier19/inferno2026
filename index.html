Award Structure

Competition Levels

Levels are based on technical ability and execution of movements, not just class time.

Novice Level 12 & Under Only
For dancers with limited technical training focused on basic foundational movements, taking no more than 3 technique classes per week. Example skills: single pirouette, chaîné turns, piqué turns, and simple leaps. Limited to one solo per event.
Intermediate Level
Dancers taking no more than 5 technique classes per week who can execute basic skills and begin some advanced movements. Example skills: double pirouettes, grand jetés, maxi ford turns, double stag leaps, arabesque pitch, and attitude positioning. Dancers may choose to perform up in the Competitive level to be considered for Scholarship Vouchers and Titles.
Competitive Level
Dancers are expected to be technically aware of everything they perform, with original/creative choreography and polished, well-rehearsed performances. Example skills: triple pirouettes, leg hold turns, switch leaps, fouetté turns, turns à la seconde, calypsos, illusions. Eligible for Regional and National Titles, and overalls including medals, banners, and cash vouchers.
Adult Level
For routines with an average age of 20 or older. Routines will be adjudicated but are not eligible for Overall Awards.

Note: We do not have any dancers in the Adult category at this event.

Overall Awards
Novice, Intermediate, and Competitive routines receive overall awards at each session per age group (Mini, Child, Junior, Teen, and Senior).

Competitive Overalls: Soloists awarded up to 10th place (20+ dancers). All other categories up to 5th place. Must earn Elite Gold or High Gold.

Intermediate Overalls: Soloists awarded up to 10th place (20+ dancers). All other categories up to 5th place (10+ routines) or 3rd place (fewer than 10). Must earn Elite Gold, High Gold, or Gold.

Novice Overalls: Soloists awarded up to 5th place (20+ dancers). All other categories up to 3rd place. All Novice routines are eligible regardless of score.

High Score awards go to the highest-scoring group routine in each level (Novice, Intermediate, Competitive). Winners receive a Platinum Ticket to compete at the Platinum Circle at Nationals.

The ADCC Studio of Excellence is a staff-choice award honoring sportsmanship, kindness, dedication to education, and overall excellence. All regional winners are put to an online vote for Studio of the Year, which includes a cash prize and banner.

Titles
No additional fee required. Soloists must compete in the Competitive level and achieve an Elite Gold or High Gold score. Title winners are judges’ choice. Intermediate level dancers are not considered for Title.
