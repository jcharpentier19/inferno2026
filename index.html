Award Structure

Competition Levels

Levels are determined by the level of the skills within the routine, not by those of the individual dancer(s).

SPARK Beginner
For dancers performing routines with foundational, beginner-level skills.
FIRE Intermediate
For dancers performing routines with intermediate-level skills, executing more advanced technique and movements.
BLAZE Advanced
For dancers performing routines at the highest, most advanced technical level, with polished and well-rehearsed performances.
EMBER Different Abilities / Accessible
For dancers with physical or developmental disabilities. A separate, supportive category dedicated to celebrating every dancer.
Age Divisions
The average age of all performers is taken as of January 1, 2026, dropping any decimal. Example: an 11.8 year old dancer would compete in the 11 year old category.

Scoring System
Each routine is scored out of 300 points across three categories:
  • Execution of Technique — 35 pts (Transition, Placement & Movement: 20 / Technical Skills: 15)
  • Execution of Performance — 30 pts (Projection & Performance: 20 / Uniformity & Use of Stage: 10)
  • Artistry & Innovation — 35 pts (Dynamics & Texture: 15 / Style & Expression: 15 / Choreography & Concept: 5)

The 20 & Over division is not eligible for high score, specialty, or title awards. Ties are broken at the discretion of the on-site judging panel.

Special Awards
  • Inferno Showcase
  • Specialty / Judges' Choice
  • Best Improv (11 & Under)
  • Best Improv (12 & Over)
  • Stellar Impact Award
  • Staff Choice Pins

Inferno Showcase: Stand-out routines selected by the judges to perform in the Inferno Showcase — chosen on entertainment and creativity, not adjudicated scores.

Specialty / Judges' Choice: Awarded several times per competition to the judges' standout routines, regardless of score.

Stellar Impact Award: Honors loyal studios demonstrating positivity, kindness, and support. A $250 donation is made to a non-profit in their honor.

Staff Choice Pins: Awarded to dancers who demonstrate kindness, courteousness, and helpfulness throughout the event.

Studio Awards
For studios with 10+ routines. Categories:
  • Fans on Fire
  • Sportsmanship
  • Artistry
  • Performance
  • Technique
  • Studio on Fire
Title Competition (Solos)
Open to all soloists regardless of skill level. Routine must receive a Platinum or higher adjudication to win. Includes a virtual pre-competition interview and an on-site improv experience covering two dance styles.
